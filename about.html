About

I'm not a doctor.
I'm the person who couldn't stop asking why.

How a stubborn curiosity about everyday habits turned into a mission to bring real Chinese medicine to people in the West.

[ portrait of Sara ]

Hi, I'm Sara.

A few years ago I kept hitting the same wall a lot of us do: I was doing everything "right" — eating clean, drinking my water, staying active — and still feeling tired, foggy and somehow off. Every test came back fine. Nobody could tell me why.

Then I fell down the rabbit hole of Traditional Chinese Medicine, and couldn't climb back out. It looked at the exact things modern health advice ignored — warmth, timing, how food is cooked, how energy moves — and suddenly the small daily habits I'd never questioned started to make sense.

From curiosity to the clinic

What began as decoding my own habits became something bigger. I went to China to shadow senior doctors in person — sitting in on real consultations, watching how they read a body, learning the classical system (the Si Sheng Xin Yuan, Huang Yuanyu's lineage) that underpins everything we do.

I want to be clear about what I am and what I'm not: I'm a TCM enthusiast and educator, not a licensed practitioner. I don't diagnose or treat. What I've built is a bridge — so the depth of these doctors' work can actually reach people here, in plain English, made for modern life.

"Real Chinese medicine isn't mystical. It's a 2,000-year-old, deeply practical way of reading the body — it just never got translated for people like us."

What YinYang Well is

YinYang Well pairs that classical knowledge with real, senior doctors who review every plan — and presents it the way I wish someone had explained it to me: clear, warm, and genuinely usable. No fear, no jargon, no extreme protocols. Just an honest read of where your core vitality is, and small changes that bring it back.
