Why your meal-deal lunch leaves you bloated by 3pm

By Sara · 4 min read · Traditional Chinese Medicine, decoded

[ supermarket meal deal · cold sandwich, crisps, iced drink ]

It's the most normal lunch in Britain. A cold sandwich, a packet of crisps, an ice-cold drink — the £3.50 meal deal, grabbed on the way through Tesco, Sainsbury's, M&S, wherever you are. And if you've ever felt bloated, foggy and flat by mid-afternoon despite eating something "light", this little habit might have more to do with it than you'd think.

Here's the thing: from a Traditional Chinese Medicine perspective, the problem isn't the calories, or even really the sandwich. It's the temperature.

Your body digests with warmth

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, digestion isn't just chemistry — it's a warm process. There's an idea at the centre of it called your core vitality: the warm engine in your middle that turns food into usable energy. Think of it as a small, steady fire. When it's strong, food gets broken down cleanly and you feel light and clear afterwards.

Now hand that fire a fridge-cold sandwich and an icy drink. Before your body can use any of it, it has to warm the whole lot up — spending its own warmth to do it. Do that at one meal and you'd never notice. Do it every single lunchtime, for years, and the fire starts to dim.

In one line: cold food makes your body spend the very warmth it needs to digest. A little, every day.

What it looks like over time

You can see the pattern in people who eat cold and "clean" every day. Not dramatic illness — just a consistent, low-grade set of signs:

  • Bloating by mid-afternoon, especially after a "light" lunch
  • That 3pm energy crash you blame on a bad night's sleep
  • Cold hands, dull skin, a heavy or foggy head
  • Over time, a gut that seems to pull less goodness out of even good food

None of these show up on a blood test. But together, in this tradition, they point to one thing: a digestive fire running a little too cold.

What to do instead

You don't have to give up lunch on the go — just warm it up. A hot soup, something cooked, even warming a sandwich through. The point isn't restriction; it's temperature. Your core vitality doesn't care that it's July — it wants warmth all year round.

And if you're nodding along to that whole list above, it's worth knowing this is exactly the kind of pattern a constitution read is built to spot — and to give you a plan for.
