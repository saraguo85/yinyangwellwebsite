How It Works

A different question:
not what's broken, but what's out of balance.

A plain-English guide to how Traditional Chinese Medicine actually reads the body — and why it sees the small things modern health advice misses.

Western medicine is brilliant at finding what's broken — a marker out of range, a part that's failed. But a lot of us live in the gap it doesn't have words for: every test is "normal", yet you're tired, bloated, foggy, cold. Off.

Traditional Chinese Medicine lives in exactly that gap. It doesn't wait for something to break. It reads the whole system as it runs day to day, and asks a simpler question: is the energy flowing, and is there enough warmth to move it?

Your core vitality — the warm engine in the middle

At the centre of it all is what we call your core vitality (in the classics, the central qi): the inner fire in your middle that turns food, rest and movement into usable energy. When it's strong, things flow — you digest well, sleep deep, feel warm and clear. When it dims, the whole system slows: food sits heavy, damp builds, you feel sluggish and cold no matter what you eat.

Most modern habits — cold food, iced drinks, skipped breakfasts, late nights — quietly put that fire out. Not dramatically. Just a little, every day.

The four ideas that explain almost everything

1 · Warmth vs cold

Digestion is a warm process — your body has to gently "cook" food before it can use it. Cold, raw, fridge-temperature food makes it spend its own warmth first. Do that daily and the fire runs down.

2 · Yin & yang — the cold/slow and the warm/active

Not mystical: just the balance between the still, cooling, slowing side of you and the warm, moving, active side. Pile on the cold and slow, and everything downshifts.

3 · Damp — heavy, stuck, waterlogged

When the system can't move things along, they pool — as a heavy, sluggish, "waterlogged" state. That's the puffiness, the brain fog, the constant throat-clearing nobody connects to their lunch.

4 · Flow — everything turning in a circle

Health, in this view, is one constant flow — energy rising, circulating, settling, day and night. Stagnation is when it gets stuck. Most of what we do either keeps that circle turning, or quietly jams it.

Why this is good news

Because almost none of it requires anything extreme. If the problems come from cold, stagnation and a dimmed inner fire, the fixes are warm, simple and daily: cooked over raw, warm over iced, rhythm over chaos, small changes matched to your pattern. That's all YinYang Well really does — read where your core vitality is, and help you bring it back.
